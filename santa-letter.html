Dear Friend,

The elves have finally gone to bed, the last ribbon has been tied, and I've settled into my favourite chair by the fire — the one that smells of pine and cinnamon and very old leather — to write to you before the night begins.

I write every year, you know. Not just to children. I write to the grandmother who knitted mittens for every person on her street without telling anyone. To the neighbour who shovelled three driveways at dawn before his own. To the friend who showed up with soup, and to the parent who held it together on the days they thought they couldn't.

I see more than gifts under trees. I see the quiet acts — the card tucked into a lunch box, the letter slipped into a mailbox, the phone call on the birthday that almost got away. I see the people who make other people feel remembered.

That is the real magic. Not the sleigh, not the reindeer (though Rudolph would disagree). It's the moment someone holds something in their hands and thinks: they thought of me.

Merry Christmas, dear friend. The world is kinder because of people like you.
